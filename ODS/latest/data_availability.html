TED XML Schema Notice Data

The format of schema XML data has changed over the years in line with changes in the legislation. "Schema" refers to the fields required by law in the notice form and therefore to the data collected by the notice.

  • Schema 2.08 complies with Defence Directive 2009/81/EU and applies since 2009 to the current day to notices published using forms 16, 17, 18, and 19 pertaining to defence related purchases. The current schema version is R2.0.8.S25.

  • Schema 2.09 complies with the following directives and applied to notices published from 2016 until 1 February 2024 (non-EU Institutions) and 28 June 2024 (EU Institutions), when the transition period from schema 2.09 to eForms ended:

    • Directive 2014/24/EU repealing Directive 2004/18/EC,

    • Directive 2014/25/EU repealing Directive 2004/17/EC,

    • Directive 2014/23/EU,

    • Regulation (EC) No 1370/2007.

  • The eForms schema was implemented on the 14 November 2022 and applies to public procurement from this date. The transition period where two versions of notice data applied, schema 2.09 and eForms, was from the 14 November 2022 to the 28 June 2024.

XML Schema Data is available from 1993 to the present day.

Legislation affects data

The legislation, updated from time to time (here the link to the Regulation (EU) 2019/1780 that applies from the 14 November 2022), sets out which forms must be used in public procurement procedures, and the mandatory and optional fields that must be provided and filled in in the public procurement forms. These fields contain data that are eventually available for query. Although complex, the detail is useful in discovering which data are available for query.

As these change slightly with amendments in legislation, the exact fields might not be carried through from the beginning of data collection to now, or their class names might have changed. Fields might have been added and removed along the way.

For an overview of what data can be extracted from a notice in its current form, you can also use the notice search facility available on the TED website. Choose "Advanced Search" (All Notices), then the type of notice you are interested in, and then look at the contents of a notice from the list returned.
